Flower Crops and Diseases

Rose

Roses are susceptible to several diseases including:

  • Black Spot: Black spots with yellow halos on leaves.
  • Powdery Mildew: White powdery fungal growth on leaves and stems.
  • Rust: Orange pustules on undersides of leaves.

Control Measures:
To control these diseases, consider:

  • Using resistant varieties of roses.
  • Applying fungicides according to recommended schedules.
  • Ensuring proper air circulation and sunlight exposure.
  • Removing and destroying affected plant parts.

Marigold

Marigolds commonly face the following diseases:

  • Alternaria Leaf Spot: Dark, concentric lesions on leaves.
  • Powdery Mildew: White, powdery fungal growth on leaves.
  • Root Rot: Wilting and rotting of roots caused by soil-borne fungi.

Control Measures:
Effective control methods include:

  • Using disease-free seeds and soil.
  • Applying fungicides preventively.
  • Ensuring proper drainage to prevent waterlogging.
  • Practicing crop rotation with non-host plants.

Jasmine

Jasmine is vulnerable to various diseases, such as:

  • Leaf Blight: Dark, irregular lesions on leaves.
  • Fusarium Wilt: Yellowing and wilting of leaves due to fungal infection.
  • Sooty Mold: Black, sooty growth on leaves and stems.

Control Measures:
Recommended control strategies include:

  • Planting disease-resistant jasmine varieties.
  • Applying fungicides during critical growth stages.
  • Ensuring good air circulation around plants.
  • Regularly cleaning leaves to remove mold.

Hibiscus

Hibiscus can suffer from diseases such as:

  • Leaf Spot: Small, dark spots on leaves.
  • Root Rot: Wilting and yellowing of leaves caused by root infection.
  • Botrytis Blight: Gray mold on flowers and buds.

Control Measures:
Effective management practices include:

  • Using disease-resistant hibiscus varieties.
  • Applying fungicides at early stages of disease development.
  • Ensuring proper soil drainage.
  • Removing and destroying affected plant parts.

Chrysanthemum

Chrysanthemums are prone to diseases like:

  • Leaf Spot: Brown spots with yellow halos on leaves.
  • Powdery Mildew: White powdery fungal growth on leaves and stems.
  • Rust: Orange pustules on leaves and stems.

Control Measures:
Effective strategies include:

  • Using disease-resistant chrysanthemum varieties.
  • Applying fungicides preventively during critical growth stages.
  • Ensuring proper spacing for good air circulation.
  • Removing and destroying infected plant debris.

Lotus

Lotus can be affected by diseases such as:

  • Leaf Blight: Brown spots with yellow margins on leaves.
  • Root Rot: Wilting and yellowing of leaves due to root infection.
  • Anthracnose: Dark, sunken lesions on leaves and stems.

Control Measures:
Strategies to manage these diseases include:

  • Choosing disease-resistant lotus varieties.
  • Applying fungicides during wet periods.
  • Ensuring proper water management to prevent waterlogging.
  • Removing and destroying affected plant parts.
