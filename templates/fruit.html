Fruit Crops and Diseases

Apple

Apples can be affected by diseases such as:

  • Apple Scab: Fungal disease causing dark lesions on leaves and fruit.
  • Fire Blight: Bacterial disease causing blackened twigs and cankers.
  • Cedar Apple Rust: Fungal disease with orange spots on leaves and fruit.

Control Measures:
Strategies to manage these diseases include:

  • Planting disease-resistant apple varieties.
  • Applying fungicides during critical periods of growth.
  • Pruning to improve air circulation.
  • Removing and destroying infected plant parts.

Orange

Oranges are susceptible to diseases like:

  • Citrus Canker: Bacterial disease causing lesions on leaves and fruit.
  • Greasy Spot: Fungal disease with oily spots on leaves.
  • Anthracnose: Fungal disease causing dark lesions on fruit and leaves.

Control Measures:
Effective management practices include:

  • Using disease-resistant orange varieties.
  • Practicing good orchard sanitation.
  • Applying copper-based fungicides preventively.
  • Removing and destroying infected plant debris.

Banana

Bananas can suffer from diseases such as:

  • Panama Disease: Soil-borne fungal disease causing wilting and death.
  • Black Sigatoka: Fungal disease with black streaks on leaves.
  • Anthracnose: Fungal disease causing sunken lesions on fruit.

Control Measures:
Strategies to manage these diseases include:

  • Planting disease-resistant banana cultivars.
  • Practicing crop rotation with non-host crops.
  • Applying fungicides during rainy seasons.
  • Using clean planting material.

Grape

Grapes are vulnerable to diseases such as:

  • Powdery Mildew: Fungal disease causing white powdery growth on leaves and fruit.
  • Downy Mildew: Fungal disease with grayish-brown fuzz on undersides of leaves.
  • Black Rot: Fungal disease causing black spots on leaves and fruit.

Control Measures:
Effective management practices include:

  • Choosing disease-resistant grape varieties.
  • Practicing good canopy management.
  • Applying fungicides preventively.
  • Removing and destroying infected plant parts.

Strawberry

Strawberries can be affected by diseases like:

  • Gray Mold: Fungal disease causing fuzzy gray mold on fruit and leaves.
  • Anthracnose: Fungal disease with dark lesions on leaves and fruit.
  • Verticillium Wilt: Fungal disease causing wilting and death of plants.

Control Measures:
Strategies to manage these diseases include:

  • Planting disease-resistant strawberry varieties.
  • Practicing crop rotation with non-host crops.
  • Applying fungicides preventively during flowering.
  • Improving air circulation in the field.

Peach

Peaches are susceptible to diseases such as:

  • Peach Leaf Curl: Fungal disease causing distorted leaves and red blisters.
  • Brown Rot: Fungal disease causing brown, mummified fruit and blossom blight.
  • Scab: Fungal disease with dark lesions on leaves and fruit.

Control Measures:
Effective management practices include:

  • Using disease-resistant peach varieties.
  • Applying fungicides during critical growth stages.
  • Pruning to improve air circulation.
  • Removing and destroying infected plant parts.
