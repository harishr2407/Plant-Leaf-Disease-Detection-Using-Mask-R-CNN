Pulse Crops and Diseases

Lentils

Lentils are susceptible to diseases including:

  • Anthracnose: Dark, sunken lesions on stems, leaves, and pods.
  • Ascochyta Blight: Small, circular lesions with gray centers on leaves.
  • Rust: Reddish-brown pustules on leaves and stems.

Control Measures:
To manage these diseases, consider:

  • Planting disease-resistant lentil varieties.
  • Applying fungicides preventively during periods of high humidity.
  • Practicing crop rotation to reduce disease pressure.
  • Removing and destroying crop debris after harvest.

Chickpeas

Chickpeas commonly suffer from diseases such as:

  • Ascochyta Blight: Dark, irregular spots on leaves and stems.
  • Botrytis Gray Mold: Grayish-brown mold on pods and stems.
  • Fusarium Wilt: Yellowing and wilting of leaves due to fungal infection.

Control Measures:
Effective control methods include:

  • Using disease-resistant chickpea cultivars.
  • Applying fungicides during flowering and pod development.
  • Practicing crop rotation with non-host crops.
  • Managing irrigation to avoid water stress.

Peas

Peas are vulnerable to diseases such as:

  • Powdery Mildew: White, powdery growth on leaves and stems.
  • Downy Mildew: Grayish mold on the undersides of leaves.
  • Pea Wilt: Wilting and yellowing of leaves due to bacterial infection.

Control Measures:
Strategies to manage these diseases include:

  • Choosing resistant pea varieties.
  • Practicing crop rotation with non-legume crops.
  • Applying fungicides preventively in humid conditions.
  • Improving air circulation and reducing plant density.

Beans

Beans can suffer from diseases such as:

  • Angular Leaf Spot: Angular lesions with a water-soaked appearance on leaves.
  • Rust: Reddish-brown spores on leaves and stems.
  • Common Blight: Dark, sunken lesions on leaves and pods.

Control Measures:
Effective management strategies include:

  • Planting disease-resistant bean varieties.
  • Practicing crop rotation and avoiding planting beans in wet fields.
  • Applying fungicides during flowering and pod development.
  • Removing and destroying infected plant debris.

Soybeans

Soybeans are susceptible to diseases such as:

  • Soybean Rust: Orange to reddish-brown pustules on leaves, stems, and pods.
  • Sudden Death Syndrome: Sudden wilting and death of plants, often after flowering.
  • Phytophthora Root Rot: Brown lesions on roots leading to rotting and plant decline.

Control Measures:
Effective strategies include:

  • Planting resistant soybean varieties.
  • Using seed treatments to protect against early-season infections.
  • Practicing crop rotation with non-host crops like corn or wheat.
  • Applying fungicides and managing irrigation to reduce disease pressure.

Fava Beans

Fava beans can be affected by diseases such as:

  • Chocolate Spot: Brown spots with white centers on leaves and pods.
  • Rust: Orange pustules on leaves and stems.
  • Ascochyta Blight: Small, circular lesions with gray centers on leaves and stems.

Control Measures:
Strategies to manage these diseases include:

  • Choosing disease-resistant fava bean varieties.
  • Practicing crop rotation with non-legume crops.
  • Applying fungicides preventively during wet periods.
  • Monitoring and removing infected plant debris.
